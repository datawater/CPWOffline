Green Light Chess, (Green Light, GLChess [2])
a WinBoard compliant chess engine by Tim Foden, written in C++, version 2.09 relying on an own 32-bit Windows GUI [3].

Description

Green Light started its life in early 1997 as 0x88 engine, and since version 2.xx evolved to bitboards with an 8x8 board. It applies Alpha-Beta inside an iterative deepening framework with fractional ply extensions, transposition table, null move reductions, razoring, and pruning in quiescence search based on SEE. In version 2.14, TD-λ learning was implemented which required milli-pawn values rather than centi-pawn values. The well maintained 2.18 change log gives a decent overview of features, modifications and bugfixes within the engine development, its GUI and WinBoard implementations [4].

Tournament Play

Green Light Chess felt well in the green-light flooded cave in Graz and played a strong WCCC 2003 with a respectable fifth place and 6 points out of 11, and further played the CCT6 with 5½ out of 9.
