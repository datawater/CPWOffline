Work

Peter Dayan's work has been influential in several fields impinging on cognitive science, including machine learning, mathematical statistics, neuroscience and psychology - he has articulated a view in which neural computation is akin to a Bayesian inference process [6]. His research centers around self-supervised learning, reinforcement learning, temporal difference learning, population coding and Monte-Carlo tree search. He researched and published on Q-learning with Chris Watkins [7], and provided a proof of convergence of TD(Î») for arbitrary Î» [8].

Learning Go

Along with Nicol N. Schraudolph and Terrence J. Sejnowski, Peter Dayan worked and published on temporal difference learning to evaluate positions in Go [9] [10].
