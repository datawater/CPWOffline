Sune Fischer [1]

Sune Fischer,
a Danish physicist, computer scientist and full-stack .NET developer with experience and interests in solution architecture, cloud computing, code quality, agile processes, automated testing and CI\CD\DevOps [2]. He holds a Bachelor in physics in 2001 from University of Copenhagen on simulated annealing and travelling salesman problem, covering Metropolis–Hastings algorithm, thermodynamics and statistical mechanics, and further worked on neural networks. As a chess player and computer chess programmer, Sune Fischer is author of the Chess Engine Communication Protocol and UCI compatible chess engine Frenzee, previously known as ChessCraft [3]. Along with Pradu Kannan, he contributed to the Chess Programming Wiki in 2007 with the examination on Pawn Advantage, Win Percentage, and Elo.
