Stefan Meyer-Kahlen [1]

Stefan Meyer-Kahlen,
a German computer scientist, professional computer chess programmer, and author of Shredder [2]. In the early 90s Stefan started chess programming and co-authored XXXX in cooperation with his fellow Martin Zentner. He began to develop his own program Shredder in early 1995. After winning the WMCCC 1996 in Djakarta, he started his professional career. Along with SOS author Rudolf Huber, Stefan Meyer-Kahlen designed the UCI protocol, released in 2000 [3].
