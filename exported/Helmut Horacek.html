Helmut Horacek [1]

Helmut Horacek,
an Austrian computer scientist, mathematician and AI-researcher with focus on natural language processing affiliated with the German Research Centre for Artificial Intelligence, Saarbr√ºcken [2], and Saarland University. During the early 80, along with Hermann Kaindl, Marcus Wagner and Roland Schreier, Helmut Horacek was co-author of the chess program Merlin, competing at various ACM North American Computer Chess Championships and two World Computer Chess Championships in the 80s [3]. He is further author of the Pawn Endgame program PawnKing, which was also subject of his Ph.D. thesis, co-supervised by Wilhelm Barth from Vienna University of Technology and Curt Christian from University of Vienna [4]. As elaborated in his 1983 paper [5], PawnKing relies on pawn- and king-pattern with bitboards as well as path algorithms to guide the search in his knowledge-based move selection and evaluation approach.
