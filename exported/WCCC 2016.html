Snellius Building with tournament room [1]

22nd World Computer Chess Championship 2016, from June 27 to July 01, 2016, Snellius Building [2] [3], Leiden University, Leiden, The Netherlands, in conjunction with the WCSC 2016, the CG 2016 [4], and the 19th Computer Olympiad [5] . Six participants played a double round robin, time control is 105 minutes per side plus 15 seconds increment per move (105+15). Komodo by Don Dailey, Larry Kaufman and Mark Lefler, operated by Erdogan Günes and defending champion Jonny by Johannes Zwanzger finished first with 7½ / 10 followed closely by Shredder by Stefan Meyer-Kahlen with 7 / 10. In a nerve-racking play-off with 2 x 45+15, 2 x 5 + 5 and 2 x 3 + 5 time controls, and five draws, Komodo won the sixth game to become deserved World Computer Chess Champion 2016.
