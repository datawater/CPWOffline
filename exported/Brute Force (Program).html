Brute Force,
a chess program by Louis Kessler that participated in the 8th and 9th North American Computer Chess Championships in 1977 and 1978. Brute Force was a Tech like program, including such features of an iterative alpha-beta routine, a quiescence routine using swap-off values, and an evaluation function based on the opponent's mobility [2]. At the 9th NACCC, it suffered from an "en passant bug" twice [3], which turned out a wrong implementation due to Kessler's misinterpretation of the rules [4].
