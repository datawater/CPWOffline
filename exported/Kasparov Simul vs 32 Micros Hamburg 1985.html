Kasparov Simul vs 32 Micros Hamburg 1985,
a simultaneous exhibition of Garry Kasparov playing 32 chess computers in Hamburg, June 06, 1985, organized by the German weekly magazine Der Spiegel, Computerschach und Spiele and ChessBase [1] . He won with a perfect 32-0 score. The computers were dedicated units, except two experimental Mephisto entries, Genius 68000 and Rebell Experimental which ran on general purpose computers operated by their authors Richard Lang and Ed Schröder. There were eight entries each by Hegener & Glaser, Fidelity Electronics, Novag and SciSys with programs by Ulf Rathsman, Lang and Schröder, Dan and Kathe Spracklen, David Kittinger, and Julio Kaplan and Craig Barnes.

Quotes

According to Kasparov, one Turbostar 432 might have missed the win with 37...Qa4 instead of Qb3 [3] . Kasparov's quote in Chess Metaphors: Artificial Intelligence and the Human Mind by Diego Rasskin-Gutman on that game [4] [5] : 

At one point I realized that I was drifting into trouble in a game against one of the “Kasparov” brand models. If this machine scored a win or even a draw, people would be quick to say that I had thrown the game to get PR for the company, so I had to intensify my efforts. Eventually I found a way to trick the machine with a sacrifice it should have refused. From the human perspective, or at least from my perspective, those were the good old days of man vs. machine chess.
