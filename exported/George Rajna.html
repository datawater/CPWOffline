George Rajna [1]

George Rajna,
a Hungarian-Israeli physicist, computer scientist, and Chess International Master, in 1987 among the Top 100 players of the world [2]. In the 70s, while affiliated with the Eötvös Loránd University and the Hungarian Academy of Sciences, he co-authored the chess program Papa (along with B. Almasi), which participated at the 1st World Computer Chess Championship in 1974 [3]. In the early 90s he researched at Tel Aviv University on artificial intelligence and computer chess [4].
