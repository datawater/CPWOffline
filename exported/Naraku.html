Naraka [1]

Naraku,
an UCI compatible chess engine by Marco Meloni, written in C, first released in July 2010. Naraku is a bitboard engine, applying standard search techniques such as alpha-beta with transposition table inside an iterative deepening framework, enhanced by null move pruning, futility pruning, late move reductions and quiescence search [2]. Naraku had a strong tournament debut, winning TCEC Season 2, Division F in February 2011. After clone suspicions appeared [3], and admitting taking ideas from Fruit, Crafty, Ippolit (bitboard infrastructure) and Stockfish, the author stopped working on Naraku [4].

Etymology

The name of the engine is borrowed from the main antagonist of the Inuyasha Japanese manga series, Naraku (奈落, Naraku, litt. Naraka, Hell), a half-demon responsible for the misery of most of the main cast [5].
