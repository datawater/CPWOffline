A Maverick [1]

Maverick,
an UCI compatible open source chess engine written by Steve Maughan in C++. The development started in March 2013 as documented in Steve's Computer Chess Programming blog [2] , the first version was already released in August 2013 [3].

Features

Maverick features plain magic bitboards to determine sliding piece attacks, and multiple slot transposition table, IID, NMP, ETC and a few extensions inside the iterative deepening loop processing the root. Move ordering considers MVV/LVA for captures and killers and history counters otherwise. Quiescence search tries all SEE positive captures, and checks at its first ply. Maverick's tapered evaluation focuses on material, piece-square tables, mobility and passed pawns, and automated tuning [4] is planned for future versions.

Tournament Play

Maverick had its over the board tournament debut at the WCCC 2015 and the WCSC 2015 in Leiden, where it managed to drew Komodo at the WCSC [5].
