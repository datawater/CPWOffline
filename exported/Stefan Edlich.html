Stefan Edlich [1]

Stefan Edlich,
a German computer scientist and engineer, and senior lecturer at the University of Applied Science, Beuth Hochschule f√ºr Technik Berlin. His interests include NoSQL Web 2.0 and software engineering, cloud computing, data-science, and programming languages, such as Java, Ruby and D, and chess programming [2]. He is author of some experimental chess playing entities, the private or no longer available EdlChess, written in Ruby [3] [4] as well as Rasbojnik, written in D [5] [6], and most recently, the UCI compliant open source chess engine and random mover Finny, written in Python 3 using the Python-chess library [7].
