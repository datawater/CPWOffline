John Nunn [1]

John Denis Martin Nunn,
a British former world top ten (1989 rating of 2620) chess grandmaster [2], chess composer and three-time world champion in chess problem solving, chess writer, publisher and director of Gambit Publications, mathematician with a Ph.D. in 1978 at the age of 23 on algebraic topology [3], and polymath. He has been involved in information extraction and data mining from endgame tablebases, as lectured at the Advances in Computer Chess 7 conference [4] and published in the ICCA Journal [5]. Concerning computer chess, John Nunn served as tournament director at the 4th European MCC 1983, and played against chess programs at four Aegon Tournaments, 1992, 1993 (tied with winner David Bronstein), 1994 (tied with winner Larry Christiansen), and 1995. His 10 and later 20 opening proposals as published by Computerschach und Spiele were used in engine testing and matches [6]. In 2013 [7], Nunn applied the 7-piece Lomonosov Tablebases to R+2P vs. R+P positions from the famous book Rook Endings, 2nd edition, by Levenfish and Smyslov [8], which was assumed to contain the truth and, owing to Nunn, this is no longer so.
