Ktulu,
a commercial UCI and WinBoard compatible chess engine by Rahman Paidar distributed by Lokasoft, but as of July 2020 no longer available [2]. Ktulu has a sophisticated evaluation - King safety and passed pawn evaluation, completely rewritten in version 6.2, are responsible for its dangerous attacking style with strong tactical and positional play. Further, some unsafe pruning algorithms were replaced with more sound methods [3]. The first version of Ktulu was written in early 2002 in Turbo Pascal under MS-DOS. The development of the Windows version started in summer 2002 [4].

Tournament Play

Ktulu played four CSVN tournaments over the board, the Leiden DOCCC 2005, DOCCC 2008, ICT 2009 and DOCCC 2009, operated by Hans Secelle. Ktulu furher participated at the CCT10, CCT11 and CCT12 online tournaments, and the WCRCC 2007 - the first ACCA World Computer Rapid Chess Championship.
