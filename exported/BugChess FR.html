BugChess,
a family of chess programs written by programmer François Karr supported by chess advisor Jean-Philippe Karr. The early Bugchess versions starting in 1996 running under MS-DOS [2] and Windows were compiled with Borland C++ [3] and had their own GUI.

BugChess2

BugChess2 is a WinBoard compliant chess engine written in C++. It was a complete rewrite using Bitboards [5] and has no code in common with BugChess, and features a parallel search to utilize multiple processors. BugChess2 was first released in May 2006, and played a strong WCRCC 2008 Annual ACCA World Computer Rapid Chess Championship.
