Gargamella
a WinBoard compliant chess engine written by Nicola Rizzuti, first released in December 2001 [2]. Gargamella played the CIPS 2002 Campionato Italiano per Programmi Scacchistici with 3/6.

Etymology

The programs name was inspired by Gargamella, Italian for Gargamel [3], the Evil Wizard is the sworn enemy of the Smurfs from the Belgian comic and television franchise centered on a group of small blue fictional creatures, created and first introduced as a series of comic characters by the Belgian comics artist Peyo in 1958 [4], published by Dupuis. Further, Gargamella is a genus of sea slugs, dorid nudibranchs, shell-less marine gastropod molluscs in the family Discodorididae [5].

Nicola Rizzuti about his progam [6]: 

... many  features are implemented but not activated for stability problems. I think it is better to release it than to make it get rusty on my pc and I hope that this first release will give me more speed in developing. Currently Gargamella  has a small opening book, just to vary its play and to avoid moves such as 1. a4...
