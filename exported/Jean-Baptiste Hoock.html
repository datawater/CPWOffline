Jean-Baptiste Hoock [1]

Jean-Baptiste Hoock,
a French computer scientist and engineer. He received his M.Sc. in computer science and image processing from Caen University and engineering diploma from the ENSICAEN Engineer School, Caen, France. He is working as engineer at Paris-Sud University.

Mogo

Since 2008 Jean-Baptiste Hoock is co-author of the Go playing program Mogo, using Monte-Carlo Tree Search which uses patterns in the simulations and improvements in UCT [2] .
