Milos Stanisavljevic [1]

Milos Stanisavljevic (Miloš Stanisavljević),
a Serbian electrical engineer holding a Diplom from University of Belgrade in 2004, and a Ph.D. on microelectronics from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne in 2009 [2], actually visiting scientist at IBM Zurich Research Laboratory. His professional interests include microelectronics and nanoelectronics, in particular he is researching on multi-level cell memory, his private interests include computer chess.

Computer Chess

Milos Stanisavljevic is a formidable expert of the Ippolit family of engines [3] [4], and advocate of Ippolit's originality, despite his note it used ideas from reverse engineering Rybka 3 [5]. He further stands sentinel [6] [7] [8] over a stable RobboLito release under the GNU GPL, which is due to misuse no longer available for download [9]. Further, along with Norman Schmidt, Milos Stanisavljevic worked on Fire, derived from IvanHoe and the Ippolit series of programs [10].
